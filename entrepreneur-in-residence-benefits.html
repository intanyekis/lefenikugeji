What Is An Entrepreneur-in-Residence and Why To Hire Them? Explained Here

August 05, 2024
blog

An Entrepreneur in Residence (EiR) is a person who serves as a consultant traditionally in a business setting. Usually focused on research and discovery, EiRs give new aspects of a contemporary or future idea or challenges a company may currently undergo or expects to encounter. An Entrepreneur in Residence is a subject matter expert and is often hired to work in a company, community, or country which requires the necessary abilities or expertise that would otherwise allow them to succeed in a particular business sector or market segment.  

This article describes one way to infuse innovation in a country, company, or community by outlining the types of EiR programs, and advantages of hiring an Entrepreneur-in-Residence. 

Types of Entrepreneur in Residence programs


Below are the types of the entrepreneur in residence program 

1. Within a country 

Each country and its’ culture are different. With the help of entrepreneur in residence program, teaching and sharing experiences, it reminded people that they are capable, empowered, and should take an opportunity if they want to make a difference; and ultimately that they can become who they want to be within their own country and that resources are available to complement their experience.   

2. Within a company 

a) Internal hire 

An internal Entrepreneur in Residence is a person who challenges the contemporary way of thinking and consequences the overall approach and development of a business, product, culture, etc. They promote the company to imagine like an entrepreneur; making smart choices with sometimes inadequate information; testing assumptions and learning quickly.  

Major companies like Google, Dell, and Target have EIR programs incorporated with some people or dedicated teams whose sole job is to research and consider ways to address new or current problems in innovative ways.  

b) External hire:  

By hiring an external Entrepreneur-in-Residence for some time, a company or organization can profit from a completely new, and honest opinion and perspective in business strategy and execution. Employees can be biased in their thought and decision making and scared to disagree with others if they have different opinions.  

3. Within a community 

Often as volunteers, employees of a company will give their time, knowledge, and skills to a community as an Entrepreneur-in-Residence. They will teach others about their expertise or work with a group or community program to help achieve their business or organizational objects.  

Alternatively, a community or city may invest in funding a community-centric EiR program that concentrates on brainstorming and solving queries in the community, such as becoming more technology-centric, supporting small businesses, and attracting large businesses. 

Benefits of Hiring an Entrepreneur-in-Residence 

1. Managing portfolio business 

Entrepreneurs-in-residence can direct spinoff brands and equity deals, freeing up the CEO while the business gets new market shares and audiences.  

EIRs also support founders avoid the traps inherent to become “parallel entrepreneurs,” or starting companies concurrently. With experience, you will also learn two startups basically sets one up to fail because a CEO can’t dedicate himself or herself to two all-consuming projects. However, EIRs can lift some responsibilities so that both ventures flourish.  

2. Developing key partnership strategies 

CEOs should incorporate EIRs in meetings that concern venture capital, influencers, and agencies This way, the entrepreneurs-in-residence can connect the points about portfolio businesses and hit the ground running with spinoff brands.  

3. Streamlining nitty-gritty operations  

EIRs must be given sufficient liberty to probe into the nuts and bolts of meeting clients’ requirements. Google’s EIR Jewel Burks, for instance, supports small businesses by optimizing their web efforts and giving guidance to their teams. EIRs must dig in and work at this granular level, giving the particular services customers demand.  

EIRs can also draw team members and bring them up to speed on projects as required. This will enable companies to capitalize on the strengths and expertise of their staff to determine particular issues without everyone needing to be on board from the outset.  

Not only do small businesses with EIRs advantage from having committed entrepreneurs develop unique initiatives, but they can keep their best people involved long-term.
