WaiveCar – An All-Electric Car-Sharing Service

Founded in 2016, WaiveCar is an all-electric car-sharing service based in Santa Monica. The company offers customers the opportunity to drive their cars for free for the first two hours, with a charge of $5.99 per hour thereafter. What sets WaiveCar apart from traditional car-sharing services is its unique business model, which allows it to provide this free service.

WaiveCar generates income by selling advertising space on the exteriors of its vehicles. By partnering with brands and displaying ads on the cars, WaiveCar is able to cover the costs associated with offering free rentals. This innovative approach to revenue generation has attracted attention and set WaiveCar apart from its competitors.

With its fleet of all-electric vehicles, WaiveCar not only provides an affordable alternative to traditional transportation, but also promotes sustainability. By utilizing electric cars, WaiveCar helps reduce carbon emissions and contributes to the global effort to combat climate change.

WaiveCar’s pitch on Shark Tank in 2017 undoubtedly played a significant role in garnering attention and attracting investors. The business model’s potential for success, combined with the founders’ persuasive presentation, secured a deal with Kevin O’Leary. This partnership allowed WaiveCar to secure additional funding and access to unsold ad space at a discounted rate, strengthening its financial position and providing further opportunities for growth.

WaiveCar’s commitment to affordable and sustainable transportation options, along with its innovative business model, positions the company as a key player in the car-sharing industry. With its continued focus on expansion and partnerships, WaiveCar is well-equipped to thrive in a market that increasingly values convenience, affordability, and environmental responsibility.

Competitors and Market Position of WaiveCar

WaiveCar operates in a competitive market alongside established players such as Zipcar and Car2Go. While these competitors also offer car-sharing services, WaiveCar sets itself apart with its unique advertising model, creating a distinct market position for the brand.

Zipcar, founded in 2000, is one of the pioneers in the car-sharing industry. Acquired by Avis Budget Group in 2013, Zipcar operates in over 500 cities worldwide. With a wide range of vehicles available for rent by the hour or day, Zipcar caters to individuals and businesses seeking flexible transportation options.

Car2Go, owned by Daimler AG, offers a similar service to WaiveCar. With a presence in North America, Europe, and Asia, Car2Go allows its members to access vehicles on-demand, paying by the minute, hour, or day. Car2Go aims to provide convenient and sustainable urban mobility solutions.

While these competitors have an established market presence, WaiveCar’s innovative business model has the potential to disrupt the industry. By incorporating digital advertising on their vehicles, WaiveCar not only offers a unique revenue stream but also provides advertisers with an effective way to reach their target audience. This differentiation sets WaiveCar apart from its competitors and positions it as a forward-thinking player in the car-sharing market.
