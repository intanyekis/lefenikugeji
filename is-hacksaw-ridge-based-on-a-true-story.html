Is Hacksaw Ridge Based On a True Story?

It is true that Hacksaw Ridge is based on a true story. Desmond Doss, a Seventh-day Adventist Christian who served as a combat medic in the American Army during World War II, is the subject of the movie. Doss refused to use force or kill people because he was a conscientious objector. Despite this, he volunteered to work as a medic during the Battle of Okinawa, saving the lives of nearly 75 soldiers.

The film takes some creative liberties with Doss’s story, but it is largely accurate. For example, the film shows Doss being court-martialed for refusing to carry a weapon, but this did not actually happen. However, the film does accurately portray the challenges that Doss faced as a conscientious objector in the military.
