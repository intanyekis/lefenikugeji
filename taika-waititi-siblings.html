Tweedie’s journey in the film world began with her studies at the South Seas Film & Television School.

Her commitment to Māori culture and language became evident when she worked as a language coach for the Māori production of William Shakespeare’s “Troilus and Cressida” in 2012, directed by Rachel House.

In 2017, Tweedie co-founded the production company Matewa Media alongside filmmaker Chelsea Winstanley.

This company was named in honor of Tweedie’s grandmother, Matewa Delamere, and it holds a unique mission of producing Māori language versions of Disney animated films.

Thanks to the collaboration with Disney, their first project, “Moana Reo Māori,” was released in 2018, showcasing their dedication to preserving and promoting the Māori language and culture through the magic of cinema.
